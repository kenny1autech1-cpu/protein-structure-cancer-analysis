What the structures show

FHL1 is the only family member with a disordered C-terminal tail — visible as the orange/yellow unstructured region at the end of the chain. This tail is created by alternative splicing unique to FHL1 and is absent in FHL2, FHL3, and FHL4, which are almost entirely blue (structured).

This structural difference turned out to be biologically meaningful. AlphaMissense pathogenicity scores for all 6,137 possible FHL1 missense mutations show the same boundary: mutations in the structured LIM domains (residues 1–230) are predicted damaging, while mutations in the disordered tail are tolerated. Real patient variants from ClinVar cluster in the structured core.
